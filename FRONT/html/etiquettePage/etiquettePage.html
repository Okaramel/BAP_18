Creating an Autonomous Aquaponic System

Aquaponics combines aquaculture and hydroponics, using fish waste to nourish plants. This project aims to create an easily reproducible, autonomous system with sensors to monitor the environment.

Addressing Overcrowding and Hunger Sustainably

Human overcrowding strains resources like water and food. Rapid agriculture degrades soils and ecosystems. Currently, 768 million people are undernourished. To combat hunger, we must develop local, organic farming. Aquaponics, a soil-less method, uses 80-85% less water and offers ecological benefits, helping restore food autonomy and reduce emissions through urban farming.

Optimizing Fish Selection for Aquaponics

The desired yield of plant and fish cultures determines the aquarium's size. Ideal fish have high nutritional quality, fast growth, and low environmental requirements. Domesticated fish like tilapia are preferred. This project uses Japanese goldfish and danios for their ease of care. An oxygen pump and water filtration system are added. Fish are fed with industrial food and a homemade recipe. LEDs and an automatic feeder are used.

"This project aims to create an autonomous aquaponic system that uses sensors to monitor and control the environment, showcasing various methods of soil-less agriculture."

The Future of Sustainable Food Production with Aquaponics

Aquaponics is a sustainable method that conserves water, eliminates chemical fertilizers, and offers diverse food sources with a low carbon footprint. Future improvements could include robots, tailored advice databases, and virtual reality. This innovative approach helps fight climate change.
